Siargao Island

The Ultimate Tropical Escape

Discover Southeast Asia's most sought-after tropical paradise, blending world-class surf breaks, turquoise lagoons, and laid-back island culture.

Quick Facts & Location

Location

Northeastern tip of Mindanao, Philippines

Region

Caraga

Capital

General Luna (tourism hub)

Area

437 sq km

Population

~90,000 residents

Signature Attractions

Cloud 9 Surf Break

Why go? Renowned for its perfect peeling reef break, Cloud 9 hosts international surf competitions every year.

Level: Intermediate to pro-friendly.

Tip: Best waves from September to November.

Magpupungko Rock Pools

What to see: When the tide's low, crystal-clear tidal pools appear between jagged rock formations.

Experience: Swim, cliff-jump, and snap Insta-worthy shots.

Advice: Check tide charts before heading out.

Sugba Lagoon

Highlight: Emerald waters fringed by mangroves and limestone cliffs.

Activities: Paddleboarding, kayaking, and diving from the floating cottages.

Best time: Morning light for perfect reflections.

Island-Hopping Adventures

Popular stops: Guyam, Daku, and Naked Islands.

Don't miss: Beach BBQs on Daku Island, and the powder-white sands of Naked.

Duration: Half-day to full-day tours available.

Best Time to Visit & Weather Tips

Siargao enjoys a tropical climate with two primary seasons:

Dry Season

March–October

Sunny days, ideal for surfing and beach activities.

Wet Season

November–February

Occasional rain showers; lush landscapes and fewer crowds.

Pro tip: Surf enthusiasts should plan for the "magic season" (September–October) when offshore winds and swell align for epic waves.

Getting There & Getting Around

How to Arrive

By Air

Daily flights to Sayak Airport (IAO) from Manila, Cebu, and Clark.

By Sea

Ferries from Surigao City connect to Dapa Port (2–3 hrs).

Moving Around

E-trikes

Electric tricycles are eco-friendly and cost ₱50–₱100 per ride.

Rentals

Scooters and motorbikes (₱400–₱600/day) for flexible island exploration.

Boats

Bangka tours for island-hopping and lagoon trips (₱1,500–₱2,500 per group).

Suggested 4-Day Itinerary

1

Surf & Sunset

Morning: Catch an early wave at Cloud 9 Surf Break.
Afternoon: Lunch at a beachfront café in General Luna.
Evening: Chill with a sunset cocktail at Bravo Beach Resort.
2

Tidal Wonders & Mangroves

Morning: Explore Magpupungko Rock Pools (low tide).
Noon: Seafood lunch at local cantina.
Afternoon: Kayak through Sugba Lagoon's mangrove forests.
3

Island-Hopping Extravaganza

Full day: Charter a bangka to Guyam, Daku & Naked Islands.
Highlights: Beach BBQ on Daku, snorkeling off Guyam.
Late afternoon: Return for a beachfront massage.
4

Culture & Culinary Delights

Morning: Visit Sohoton Cove (jellyfish sanctuary) on Bucas Grande Island.
Afternoon: Lunch at La Carinderia for authentic Filipino fare.
Evening: Enjoy live acoustic music at RumBar in General Luna.

Food & Nightlife Highlights

Kermit Siargao

Famous for its stone-oven pizza and pasta; perfect for group dinners.

Mama's Grill

Local hotspot for lechon kawali (crispy pork belly) and fresh salads.

Kalibu Brewery

Sample craft beers brewed with local ingredients.

RumBar

Laid-back beachfront bar with nightly acoustic sessions and fire dancers.

Sustainable Travel Pointers

Use reef-safe sunscreen

Protect coral reefs by choosing non-nano zinc or mineral-based SPF.

Hire local guides

Support community livelihoods and gain authentic insights.

Waste-free tips

Carry a reusable water bottle and say no to single-use plastics.

Eco-friendly tours

Opt for operators with environmental certifications and responsible practices.
